    • Conclusion

    It is critical to develop innovative construction materials and procedures which improve resilience to natural catastrophes in order to protect lives and infrastructure. Engineers may design and create structures and infrastructure that are more resistant to natural pressures by utilizing new materials, structural techniques, enhanced monitoring systems, and computer modeling. Collaboration among researchers, policymakers, and industry experts is also critical to driving the adoption of resilient techniques and ensuring a safer and more sustainable built environment for future generations.
