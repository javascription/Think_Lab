    • Innovative Materials

    ◆ Fiber-reinforced composites: Advanced composites, including fiber-reinforced polymers (FRPs), provide improved strength-to-weight ratios and corrosion resistance. They may be utilized to produce lightweight, sturdy components as well as adapt old buildings and strengthen concrete structures

    ◆ Self-healing materials: Materials having self-healing properties are being developed by scientists, including self-healing concrete. These substances have tiny healing agent microcapsules that can patch up cracks on their own, enhancing the resilience and lifetime of structures.

    ◆ Insulating materials: Strong insulation can help structures withstand high temperatures caused by wildfires. As a result, heat transmission to the building structure is reduced. New insulating materials offer improved fire resistance while keeping thermal insulation capabilities.

    ◆ High-performance concrete: High-performance concrete (HPC) demonstrate enhanced strength, durability, and resistance to fractures thanks to additives and optimal mix designs. The seismic performance and resistance to impact forces of buildings may both be improved by HPCs.
