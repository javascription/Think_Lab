    • Structural Techniques

    ◆ Base isolation: In order to reduce the transmission of seismic forces, base isolation requires putting a structure on adaptable supports that disconnect it from the ground. This method greatly increases a building's capacity to resist earthquakes.

    ◆ Passive damping: Installing equipment within structures, such as tuned mass dampers or viscous dampers, can absorb and distribute energy during seismic occurrences, limiting structural vibrations and damage.

    ◆ Resilient building envelopes: Using flexible, impact-resistant materials for roofs, walls, and windows helps protect structures during hurricanes and storms from strong winds, debris impact, and water infiltration.

    Advanced Monitoring and Early Warning Systems: Integrating modern monitoring technology such as sensors, real-time data processing, and early warning systems enhances structural resilience. These technologies can identify structural flaws, monitor environmental conditions, and send out timely warnings to evacuate or take preventive steps before a calamity occurs.

    ➔ Regulatory Measures and Codes Governments and regulatory agencies play an important role in encouraging the use of resilient building materials and practices. Building codes that promote disaster resilience are implemented and enforced to ensure that structures are built to resist probable dangers.
